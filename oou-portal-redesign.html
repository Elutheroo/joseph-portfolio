Olabisi Onabanjo University Portal Redesign

Transforming an outdated university portal into a modern, intuitive experience for students, staff, and cyber café operators.

Quick Facts

Role: UX/UI Designer

Duration: 1 Month

Tools: Figma, Notion

View Prototype
Project Overview

The Problem

The portal was difficult to navigate, with an outdated and cluttered layout that frustrated users.

The Goal

Create a modern, intuitive interface improving usability for students, staff, and cyber café operators.

My Process

A focused, outcome-driven approach that balanced quick usability wins with systematic redesign of information architecture and visual language.

01

Discovery & Audit

I started with a heuristic audit and analytics review to identify the most critical usability gaps. Deliverables: audit report, annotated screenshots, and a prioritized backlog.

02

Mapping & Ideation

I mapped user journeys for students, staff, and cyber-cafe operators, ran stakeholder workshops, and sketched wireframes to validate information architecture changes. Deliverables: journey maps, low-fidelity wireframes.

03

Design & Systemization

I translated validated wireframes into high-fidelity screens and created a lightweight design system to ensure consistency across pages. Deliverables: hi-fi screens, component library, and a Figma variables snapshot.

Wireframes and UI

I iterated rapidly from paper sketches to digital wireframes, then consolidated approved patterns into high-fidelity screens and components. Below are representative artifacts showing the progression from concept to final UI.

Impact & Learnings

The redesign makes the portal cleaner, faster to navigate, and more inclusive for all user types.

Key takeaway: Even legacy systems can be revitalized with thoughtful UX improvements and modern UI practices.

Outcome

Improved navigation and clearer visual hierarchy led to easier access for students and staff.
