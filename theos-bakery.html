Theo’s Bakery

A warm, inviting e-commerce experience focused on product visibility and streamlined purchase flows.

Quick Facts

Role: UX/UI Designer

Duration: 3 Months

Tools: Figma, Adobe XD

View Prototype

Project Overview

Theo’s Bakery required an experience that prioritized product discovery and made purchasing quick and delightful on mobile and desktop. I focused on simplifying the product browsing flow and creating clear conversion points while maintaining a warm brand tone.

My Process

A research-led process focused on conversion metrics and clear product discovery patterns.

01

Research & Synthesis

I conducted a heuristic review, competitor benchmarking, and spoke with store owners to identify friction in product discovery and checkout. Deliverables: research notes and a prioritized feature list that informed scope and MVP decisions.

02

Ideation & Validation

Built quick wireframes and prototypes in Figma to validate navigation and filtering patterns with real users. Iterated based on feedback to reduce clicks to purchase and clarify product discovery.

03

Design & Polish

Delivered high-fidelity screens and a small component library to speed up handoff. Focus areas: product cards, cart flow, and accessible CTAs. I prepared an annotated Figma file for engineers to reduce implementation questions.

Results

Prototype tests showed improved clarity in product discovery and fewer steps to checkout—providing a clear roadmap for the next development sprint.

Wireframes and UI

Impact and Learning

Design changes reduced friction in discovery and checkout flows during prototype testing. The component-focused approach made handoff clear and repeatable for development, enabling a faster iteration cycle.

Outcome

Improved clarity in product listings and a simplified checkout that reduced mock checkout steps during testing.
